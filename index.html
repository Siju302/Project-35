About us!

Welcome to Ade's Diner, where flavor meets comfort in a warm, inviting atmosphere. Located in the heart of Lagos, we're passionate about creating dishes that celebrate the freshest seasonal ingredients, bringing you a delightful dining experience with every visit. Our menu features a fusion of Nigerian meals, combining traditional recipes with modern twists. Whether you're here for a cozy meal with loved ones, a celebratory gathering, or just a casual bite, we've got something for everyone. We believe in quality, sustainability, and serving food that brings people together. At Ade's Diner, our team is committed to providing excellent service and ensuring that your meal is nothing short of extraordinary. Join us for an unforgettable culinary journey, and discover why we're a local favorite!

Menu!

Starters:
Puff-Puff - Soft, deep-fried dough balls, crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside.
Chin Chin - Sweet, crunchy fried dough snacks, a popular snack in Nigerian homes.
Suya - Grilled beef skewers seasoned with spicy pepper mix, served with onions and tomatoes.
Pepper Soup - Spicy broth with fish or goat meat, seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices.

Main Dishes:
Jollof Rice - Popular rice dish cooked with tomatoes, onions, and a blend of spices.
Fried Rice - Rice stir-fried with vegetables, chicken, and shrimp.
Pounded Yam & Egusi Soup - Pounded yam served with a rich melon seed-based soup (often with spinach, goat, or beef).
Efo Riro - Spinach stew cooked with tomatoes, peppers, and meat (typically beef or fish).
Ofada Rice & Ayamase Stew - Local Nigerian rice served with spicy green pepper sauce and assorted meats.
Moi Moi - Steamed bean pudding, usually served as a side dish with rice.

Sides & Accompaniments:
Plantains (Dodo) - Fried plantains served as a side dish.
Fried Yam - Crispy fried yam served with spicy pepper sauce.
Garri - Ground cassava flour, served with soup or stew.

Drinks:
Zobo - Hibiscus drink, tangy and refreshing.
Chapman - A Nigerian cocktail made with lemonade, bitters, and soda.
Palm Wine - Traditional Nigerian alcoholic drink made from fermented palm sap.
Tropical Fruit Juice - A mix of mango, pineapple, and orange juice.

Desserts:
Akara - Fried bean cakes, crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside.
Nigerian Ice Cream - Local-style ice cream, often flavored with fruits like coconut or pineapple.
